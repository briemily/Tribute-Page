Reshma Saujani

Leading the fight to close the gender gap in technology



Reshma Saujani is the Founder and CEO of Girls Who Code, a national non-profit organization working to close the gender gap in technology.

Through its Summer Immersion Programs and Clubs, Girls Who Code is leading the movement to inspire, educate, and equip young women with the computing skills to pursue 21st century opportunities.

Started in 2012, the organization will reach more than 40,000 girls in every state by the end of 2016. This year, Girls Who Code will run 78 Summer Immersion Programs and 1500 Clubs.


Significant events in the life of Reshema Saujani:
  • 1997: graduated from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign with majors in Political Science and Speech Communication
  • 1999: recieved a Master of Public Policy from Harvard University
  • 2002: received her Juris Doctor from Yale Law School
  • 2002-2010: began her career as a attorney and advocate by working at various law, magagement, and investment firms
  • 2010: challenged the incumbent Democratic Representative of her district and became the first Indian American woman to run for U.S. Congress
  • 2012: founded Girls Who Code, a nonprofit organization that seeks to close the gender gap in technology
  • 2013: ran for the role of New York Public Advocate
  • 2015: named in Fortune Magazine's 40 Under 40 list



After seeing the gender gap in the high school computing classes she visited during her 2010 run for U.S. Congress, Reshma Saujani founded the non-profit organization, Girls Who Code, in 2012.


"By 2020, there will be 1.4 million jobs available in computing related fields. US graduates are on track to fill 29% of those jobs. Women are on track to fill just 3%."


Girls Who Code is a national non-profit that is closing the gender gap in technology by holding clubs for 6th-12th grade girls and fostering confidence, teamwork, and of course knowledge of coding.



"When we teach girls to be imperfect, and we help them leverage it, we will build a movement of young women who are brave and who will build a better world for themselves and for each and every one of us."
